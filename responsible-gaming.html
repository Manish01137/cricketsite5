First principles

Five rules batbhai999 asks you to live by

1. Bet only what you can afford to lose

If losing your stake would affect rent, food, or family expenses — that stake is too high. Step away.

2. Never chase losses

Doubling stakes after a losing run is the fastest path to a serious problem. The market doesn't owe you a recovery.

3. Set a time limit

Decide before opening the site how long you'll spend. Stick to it. Set a phone alarm if you have to.

4. Never bet drunk or emotional

Alcohol, anger, big personal news — all impair judgement. Wait 24 hours before placing any bet during these states.

5. Talk to someone

If you're hiding your betting from family or partner, that's the first warning sign. Talk to someone you trust.

6. Take regular breaks

A 7-day pause every quarter resets your relationship with the game. Use the self-exclusion tool below.

Self-assessment

Nine questions — answer honestly

If you answer "yes" to three or more, professional support is recommended.

Have you ever bet more than you could really afford to lose?

If your bet sizes have ever pushed you to skip bills, borrow money, or sell things — that's a serious red flag.

Have you needed to bet larger amounts to feel the same excitement?

Tolerance is a clinical sign of addiction in any context, including gambling.

Have you tried to stop or reduce your betting and failed?

Repeated failed attempts to quit are a strong indicator that the behaviour has crossed from habit to compulsion.

Have you become restless or irritable when trying to cut down?

Withdrawal-like symptoms in a behavioural context indicate dependence.

Have you bet to escape problems or relieve a bad mood?

Using betting as emotional regulation is one of the most predictive risk factors.

Have you returned the next day to chase losses?

Loss-chasing is the single most common pattern in problem gambling.

Have you lied to family / friends about how much you bet?

Hiding the behaviour is often the first sign someone admits the problem to themselves.

Have you committed (or considered) illegal acts to fund betting?

If yes, please seek help today — there is no shame, and free professional support is available.

Has betting damaged a relationship, job or studies?

External costs are usually visible to others before they're visible to the punter.

For family & friends

Worried about someone you love?

If you suspect a partner, parent, child or friend has a gambling problem, the most useful things you can do are:

  1. Talk to them privately, without judgement. Avoid accusations or ultimatums in the first conversation.
  2. Encourage them to call a helpline (above) — and offer to be in the room while they do. The first call is the hardest.
  3. Don't pay off their debts immediately. Counsellors widely advise that bailing out enables the behaviour. Help them find a path forward, not a quick fix.
  4. Look after yourself too. The Vandrevala Foundation (1860-266-2345) and iCall (9152987821) both offer free support to family members of problem gamblers.
  5. Email us at help@batbhai999.example — if your loved one has a batbhai999 account, we can apply a permanent self-exclusion at your written request, with the account-holder's confirmation.
